#ChessNotCheese

Depending on where you live, there’re different ways to smile for a picture.

In Bulgaria you say “Zele!”, which means cabbage. In Morocco you say “Khbiz!, which is bread. And in Sweden you say “Omelett”, which of course stands for omelette. There’s a whole kitchen out there.

But there’s one ingredient which always takes the main stage. It’s as easy to say as delicious to consume. Yes, you got it. Cheese!

Cheese has been there forever, or at least that’s how it feels every time someone’s trying to take a picture. But it’s come to our attention that there’s a new contender out there. It’s neither food nor beverage. It’s chess.

The #ChessNotCheese movement is getting lots of traction, especially in northern europe. Rumors say it all started in Copenhagen, while two friends were playing a friendly game by the lakes. They wanted a selfie to remember that marvelous moment but instead of saying cheese, they said chess.
